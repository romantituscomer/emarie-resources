10 Things to Do This Weekend

  1. Call or text 211 — ask about housing, childcare, and emergency cash in your area
  2. Apply for WIC — you likely qualify now as a pregnant woman. Find your county clinic at odh.ohio.gov
  3. Apply for Medicaid at benefits.ohio.gov — covers prenatal care and baby's first year
  4. Check USDA eligibility for a 0 dollar down home loan at eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov
  5. Browse OhioMeansJobs.com — jobs, training programs, apprenticeships
  6. Contact Habitat for Humanity in your area at habitatforhumanity.org
  7. Apply to OHFA at ohfa.org — up to 5,000 dollars closing cost assistance
  8. Find a HUD counselor for free homebuyer advice at hud.gov/counselors
  9. Look into local food banks — search your county name plus food bank — they also help with housing referrals
  10. If considering job training — ask OhioMeansJobs about free childcare while you are in training
Housing and Homeownership
Programs to help buy, rent affordably, or find housing assistance

USDA 502 Loans — 0 Dollar Down Payment

Federal loan program for low-income buyers in rural areas. Zero down, below-market rates. Most west-central Ohio counties qualify.

502 Direct: Interest rate as low as 1 percent, 33-year term. Best deal if you qualify on income.
502 Guaranteed: Through a private lender — easier, no down, market rate.
  • Check eligibility: eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov
  • Ohio RD: rd.usda.gov/oh — (614) 872-0900
  • Apply through any USDA-approved lender (most banks)

FHA Loans — 3.5 Percent Down

Federal Housing Administration backed loans. Easier to qualify with lower credit scores. Available statewide.

  • Apply through any bank or mortgage broker
  • Minimum credit score: typically 580 (some lenders go lower with 10 percent down)

OHFA — Ohio Housing Finance Agency

Ohio housing agency. Below-market mortgages plus closing cost assistance for first-time buyers.

MyCASH: Up to 5,000 dollars closing cost help for households earning under 75K
Dream 2nd Chance: For buyers with past credit challenges
  • ohfa.org — find an approved lender
  • Phone: (888) 362-6432

Framework Homeownership Course — Pays Your Closing Costs

A HUD-approved homeownership education course. Completing it through an approved counselor can unlock OHFA closing cost assistance and other benefits. Course fee is typically 75 to 125 dollars — some programs reimburse it upon completion.

How it works: Take the Framework course online, get a certificate of completion, present it to your lender or OHFA-approved counselor. This unlocks access to MyCASH closing cost assistance and shows lenders you are a serious buyer.
  • frameworkhomeownership.org or ask your OHFA-approved lender
  • Some Ohio counties reimburse the course fee upon closing — ask your lender
  • Available in English and Spanish

Habitat for Humanity

Builds homes at cost with an interest-free mortgage. No down payment. Family puts in sweat equity hours.

  • Find your affiliate: habitatforhumanity.org
  • Ohio affiliates: Columbus, Dayton, Toledo, Cincinnati, Akron, Youngstown and more

County Land Banks

Properties seized for unpaid taxes — often sold for 1 to 5 dollars. Path to homeownership in west-central Ohio.

  • Contact your county auditors office — ask about land bank programs

Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers

Federal rental assistance — pays part of rent directly to landlord. Long waitlists but worth applying.

  • Apply through your local Public Housing Authority (every county has one)
Financial Assistance and Benefits
Free money for food, utilities, healthcare, and emergencies

Ohio Benefits — One Application, 70+ Programs

One application covers SNAP (food stamps), Medicaid, HEAP, TANF, childcare, and more.

  • benefits.ohio.gov | Phone: (844) 640-6446

WIC (Women, Infants and Children)

Federal nutrition program for pregnant women, new moms, infants, children under 5. Food, nutritional guidance, breastfeeding support.

Emarie is pregnant — she likely qualifies immediately. Apply at your county health department WIC office.
  • odh.ohio.gov/know-our-programs/women-infant-children-wic-program

Medicaid — Pregnancy Coverage

Ohio Medicaid covers pregnant women at up to 200 percent of federal poverty level. Covers prenatal care, delivery, baby for the first year.

Apply immediately. Even with other insurance, Medicaid can cover deductibles and copays.
  • benefits.ohio.gov | (800) 324-8680

SNAP (Food Stamps)

Monthly grocery benefit. Apply even if you think you will not qualify — amounts change.

  • benefits.ohio.gov

HEAP (Home Energy Assistance)

One-time winter heating assistance — 300 to 1,000+ dollars for low-income households.

  • benefits.ohio.gov or county JFS
Pregnancy, Family and Health
Healthcare, prenatal support, and family services

Help Me Grow

Ohio early intervention program for pregnant women and families with young children. Home visits, health education, connections to services.

  • helpmegrow.ohio.gov | Phone: (800) 755-4769

Nurture the Next Generation

Ohio program for pregnant women and new mothers. Home visits, health education, resource connections.

  • Phone: (800) 755-4769

March of Dimes Ohio

Support groups, postpartum support, resource connections for new and expecting moms.

  • marchofdimes.org/ohio
Childcare
Subsidies, free programs, and childcare help

Ohio Childcare Subsidies (JFS)

State pays part of childcare costs for low-income working families. Apply early — waitlists can be long.

  • Apply through county JFS office
  • Pregnant women in job training often qualify

Head Start / Early Head Start

Free early childhood education for children 0 to 5 from low-income families. Includes health, nutrition, family support.

Early Head Start: Infants and toddlers (birth to 3)
Head Start: Ages 3 to 5
  • eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov — find your local agency
Jobs and Training
Free job search help, training programs, and apprenticeships

OhioMeansJobs.com

Free job search, resume help, training programs, and apprenticeships. Career counseling at no cost.

  • ohiomeansjobs.com | Phone: (888) 296-7561

Trade Apprenticeships

Earn while you learn — paid on-the-job training plus classroom instruction. No cost to the apprentice.

In-demand trades in Ohio: welding, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, CDL driving, healthcare (CNA, phlebotomy)

  • Find through ohiomeansjobs.com or local JFS office

Ohio College Opportunity Grant

For low-income Ohio students at in-state colleges. Up to 4,500 dollars per year.

  • Apply through your college financial aid office
Banking and Credit
Building credit and avoiding predatory banking

No-Fee Banking

Chime, Current, Varo — no overdraft fees, no minimum balance, free direct deposit.

  • chime.com | current.com | varomoney.com

CDFIs in Ohio

Community Development Financial Institutions — banks and credit unions that serve low-income communities.

  • Find Ohio CDFIs at ofn.org/find-a-cdfi

Self Financial (Credit Builder)

Helps build credit with a small credit builder loan. Reports to all three credit bureaus.

  • selffinancial.com
